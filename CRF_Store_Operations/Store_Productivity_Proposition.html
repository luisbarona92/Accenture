01 · Executive Summary

The question is not to optimize a little more — it is to leapfrog the ceiling

Store staff costs have plateaued after years of optimization. The point now is to make stores a real contributor to Carrefour's 2030 cost ambition.

Our recipe — from work with dozens of retailers

🎯
Put execution under control first
The biggest gains come from controlling how hours are actually spent. Make the hours budget a daily management tool.
🔧
Fix what quietly creates overwork
The biggest overcost is upstream: shelf-capacity data, planograms, promo case-ordering that piles up backroom stock.
⚖️
Decide where the hours go
Customer service level, what supply chain absorbs vs store, promotion-driven operational cost. Make these explicit choices.

BREAD traces the full chain — not just the floor, but upstream decisions that create the problem.

Corporate Layer

Buying
Order sized without shelf-capacity data or per-format traffic patterns.
Supply Chain
Wrong volumes shipped, pallets unsequenced, not aligned with shelf layout.

Store Impact

📦 Backroom
Staff must fully re-sort misaligned pallets. Hours of unplanned double-handling masked as 'slow replenishment.'
🛒 Store Floor
Empty shelves — OOS despite stock in backroom. Root cause invisible. Fixing replenishment won't solve it.
