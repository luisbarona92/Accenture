The driver logic

A business driver is a specific, countable unit of work that Accenture directly operates and can independently audit. No driver is a percentage of marketplace GMV or consortium revenue — each is a discrete operational event (a SKU updated, a campaign executed, a ticket resolved, an order routed, a licence activated). Accenture's incentive is to drive more volume of each driver — which is also what generates value for the consortium.

Four reasons the long-term Accenture partnership is the right choice
Designed for the consortium CEOs — direct, no jargon. What does signing with Accenture mean for each company?
01
You pay for specific operations — not for our time
Every Accenture fee is tied to a countable event you can audit: SKUs updated, campaigns run, orders routed, licences active. If we don't operate it, you don't pay for it.
02
We co-invest and share the downside
Accenture reduces your upfront CAPEX by co-investing in the build. If adoption is slow, our recovery adjusts proportionally. If the platform grows, our fees scale with it.
03
One platform for three separate networks
SBG, Delta, and Sumol+Compal each run different ERPs, logistics, and commercial teams. The SC Control Tower and integration layer handle this complexity. A build-only contractor doesn't price this in.
04
You own it on your own timeline
Each hub has a contractual transfer date — people, processes, and IP move to the NewCo in waves. Accenture ran this exact model with a consumer goods multinational across 12 markets. It is not theoretical.
The BOT structure

Build — Operate — Transfer: the four mechanics

Accenture Credential: from a marketplace of a multinational to Nexus
€20M co-investment, €366M over 5 years, 7 hubs, 12 markets — Build-Operate-Transfer across all of them. The contract mechanics below are not invented for Nexus. They are active, proven, and reusable.
Co-invest
Accenture invests upfront
Part of the Build fee becomes Accenture's co-investment — reducing the consortium's CAPEX requirement. Recovered as a fee discount amortised over the operating contract term.
Rollback
Shared downside protection
If billing volume doesn't reach agreed thresholds at each annual checkpoint, Accenture's investment recovery adjusts proportionally. Protects the consortium if adoption is slower than forecast.
Scale bonus
Upside tied to platform milestones
Accenture earns a defined milestone bonus when the platform reaches agreed scale checkpoints (e.g. 10,000 active HoReCa PoS, Phase 2 open to 3P sellers). Countable, binary — not GMV percentages.
Transfer
Contractual hub handover dates
Each service hub has a defined transfer date to the consortium NewCo. Accenture's team and IP migrate in waves. Platform Tech hub ~Year 3; CX and SC hubs last. The consortium builds capability progressively.
